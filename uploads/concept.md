# Sudoku Dojo — Concept

## The Core Idea

**The first Sudoku platform that rates every puzzle dynamically — and matches puzzles to your personal rating.**

Like Chess.com does for chess puzzles. Every puzzle has a rating that adjusts based on aggregate solver performance. Every player has a rating that adjusts based on how they solve. The system always knows what difficulty is right for you. No static Easy/Medium/Hard labels. A living, accurate difficulty system.

This is the one thing no mainstream Sudoku product ships. It is the defensible center of Sudoku Dojo.

---

## The Real Differentiator

**Per-puzzle dynamic Elo.** Not player-vs-player Elo (Minimal Sudoku already does that). Not static difficulty scores (Sudoku.coach uses those). A Glicko/Elo model where each individual puzzle has a rating that moves with real solver success rates — and players are matched to puzzles near their level.

Built correctly, this means:
- A puzzle rated 1400 actually feels like a 1400 to most solvers
- A player at 1200 gets puzzles that challenge but don't crush them
- Rating gains and losses feel earned, not arbitrary
- The number has meaning — it does not inflate

---

## Everything Else — Coherence, Not Novelty

The Japanese aesthetic, pen-on-paper sounds, belt ranks, Hanko stamps — none of these are novel on their own. Good Sudoku has great audio. NYT has minimalism. Sudoku.coach has technique teaching.

What no product has is all of it in one coherent, ad-free package — unified around a real rating system.

The theme is the brand. The rating is the product.

---

## The Teaching Loop (How We Beat Sudoku.coach)

The Technique Dojo is not a standalone curriculum. It is wired into the rating system:

1. Player fails several puzzles requiring X-Wing
2. System detects the pattern from rating data
3. Surfaces a targeted X-Wing lesson
4. Feeds rated puzzles that specifically exercise X-Wing
5. Rating updates reflect real improvement

This closed loop — **diagnose via rating → teach → re-test with rated puzzles** — is something neither Sudoku.coach nor Good Sudoku fully does. Teaching becomes a consequence of your data, not a separate tab you have to find.

---

## Two Modes, Two Audiences

**Rated Mode** — the competitive core. Every solve affects your rating. Puzzles matched to your level. Streaks, belts, leaderboards. For players who want to improve.

**Zen Mode** — score-free practice. No timer, no rating changes, no mistakes counted. Ambient sounds only. For players who want to relax. (Borrowed from Alto's Odyssey — a large, underserved audience in Sudoku.)

---

## Why It Wins

- **Against Sudoku.com:** Ad-free, premium feel, actual depth of progression
- **Against NYT:** A real rating system, teaching, meaningful progression
- **Against Good Sudoku:** Per-puzzle Elo, the teaching-rating feedback loop, web platform
- **Against Sudoku.coach:** Sensory quality, coherent theme, Elo system — and teaching that responds to your data rather than being a static curriculum
